# Visual Design Lens

A senior visual/brand designer's eye. Especially for "make it feel ___" requests — force
the vague feeling into concrete visual decisions.

- **Name the adjectives.** Calm? Authoritative? Playful? Premium? Pin the 2–3 target words.
- **Translate feeling → mechanics.** Each adjective maps to specific choices:
  calm = generous whitespace, muted palette, low contrast motion;
  authoritative = strong type hierarchy, restrained color, structure over decoration.
- **Type hierarchy.** Is there a clear scale (display / heading / body / caption)? Line length 45–75ch?
- **Spacing system.** Consistent spacing scale, or arbitrary pixel values? Rhythm and alignment?
- **Color discipline.** Limited palette with intent? Sufficient contrast? Semantic colors consistent?
- **Visual weight.** Does the eye land where it should? What's competing for attention?
- **Restraint.** What can be removed? Decoration that doesn't earn its place?
- **Consistency with brand.** Does it match existing brand tokens, or drift?
- **Motion.** If animated, does motion support meaning or just decorate?
